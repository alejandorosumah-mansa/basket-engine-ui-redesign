1. Scope And Design Intent

The engine is intentionally single-purpose: construct thematic baskets that are coherent, tradable, and auditable. The strategy is rules-based with no manual override path during monthly construction. Every selection, exclusion, and weight comes from deterministic code and source data.

The key design tradeoff is between thematic purity and execution realism. The process prioritizes pure theme exposure first, then applies implementability and risk controls (liquidity/history quality, diversification caps, turnover-aware cash sleeve, and contract lifecycle handling).

2. Data Universe And Temporal Realism

Universe data is loaded from data/processed/ticker_mapping.parquet. Contract chain lineage is loaded from data/processed/ticker_chains.json. Temporal listing controls are loaded from data/processed/polymarket_market_history.parquet. Pricing inputs come from data/processed/prices.parquet and data/processed/returns.parquet. The engine may use optional precomputed enrichment columns when they already exist inside the processed market universe, but it does not depend on any separate legacy clustering pipeline.

Temporal realism is enforced with two rules. First, at each rebalance date, contracts must satisfy minimum time-to-expiry. Second, if inferred first-seen information is available, contracts are excluded before their inferred market appearance date. This prevents lookahead contamination when historical snapshots are reconstructed from cumulative data dumps.

  • Minimum days-to-expiry threshold: 21 days.
  • Ticker-chain de-duplication: one selected market per ticker_id at a rebalance.
  • Certainty-input status in this snapshot: available (theme-aligned effective price and certainty rule active in selection diagnostics).

3. Candidate Filtering And Noise Rejection

Filtering is applied in layers to remove non-semantic noise before scoring. Global filters remove election campaign contracts and broad sports/entertainment contracts. Basket-specific filters then remove terms that introduce off-theme chatter. Category gating keeps contracts aligned with each basket taxonomy, while still allowing limited high-keyword fallback from unknown categories when needed for coverage.

This layered gate is the primary anti-noise control: contracts must pass theme relevance, category compatibility, and hard exclusions before they can compete for selection.

  • Global hard rejects: election/presidential/campaign and broad sports/entertainment classes.
  • Basket-level required patterns and exclusion patterns.
  • Optional certainty filter when price data exists: remove contracts with theme-aligned effective price ≤ 0.05 or ≥ 0.95.

4. Selection Logic, Diversification, And Exclusivity

After filtering, each candidate receives a multi-factor selection score that blends thematic relevance with quality and stability. The selector then runs a greedy marginal-add process, where each new contract must improve basket objective net of similarity and concentration penalties.

selection_score = 0.36*keyword_z + 0.27*quality_z + 0.10*stability_z + 0.05*trend_z + 0.08*category_z

marginal = selection_score - 0.35*max_text_similarity - template_penalty - event_penalty - exclusivity_penalty - community_penalty - factor_penalty

Diversification controls enforce limits at multiple semantic levels: template, community, event-family, and explicit mutual-exclusion groups. The mutual-exclusion layer is critical for ranking-style markets (for example, “best/top/second/third AI model”): contracts sharing the same exclusivity key cannot stack in one basket-month.

The cap vector for each basket is codified in configuration and applied mechanically at every rebalance; no discretionary exception path exists.

5. Weighting, Cash, And Rebalance Mechanics

Selected contracts are ranked by score, quality, liquidity, and stability to produce raw weights, then clipped and normalized under basket concentration caps. The investable sleeve equals 1 - cash. Cash is dynamic, not fixed, and rises under turnover and risk stress.

raw_weight = 0.25 + 0.45*rank(selection_score) + 0.22*rank(quality) + 0.18*rank(log(1+volume)) + 0.15*rank(stability)

Cash is not transaction cost. Cash is reserve allocation inside basket construction. Cost is estimated separately using turnover and expiry/certainty pressure proxies. Rebalance occurs monthly using only information available at rebalance time.

6. Lifecycle, Expiry, And Certainty Handling

Contracts naturally roll out for expiry/resolution and are replaced by current best candidates at subsequent rebalance. If price data is available, certainty-threshold exits (≥95% or ≤5%) are enforced on the held side of the contract: YES uses the raw YES price, while NO uses 1 - YES price. Proceeds remain in cash until reallocation through the normal rebalance logic.

Lifecycle diagnostics are exported as an event stream so every entry/exit has a machine-readable reason: new selection, expiry/resolution, certainty threshold, or rebalance replacement.

estimated_cost_bps = 18 + 35*turnover + 10*expiring_30d_share + 6*expiring_60d_share + 25*certainty_share

Certainty component is active only when price inputs are present.

7. Basket Inception And Display Rules

The stored basket history can begin before a basket is meaningfully investable. In those periods the basket is effectively just cash or low-coverage placeholder exposure, which produces long flat chart segments that are technically present in the raw data but economically misleading as a default view.

The dashboard therefore computes an effective inception date for each basket. The automatic rule is the first row where price_coverage_share ≥ 0.60 and cash_weight ≤ 0.25. If no row satisfies that rule, the fallback is the first date where cash_weight < 0.95. If a manual override exists in config/basket_inception_overrides.yml, that override becomes the effective inception date. Full raw history remains accessible in the website through a Full History mode.

8. Tradable NAV Publishing

The published level is now just Tradable NAV. It answers one question: “what money would the buyer have made by owning the basket through rolls, exits, costs, and cash management?”

For baskets like ADIT-S3, this keeps the chart focused on the tradable object rather than mixing it with a separate normalized risk overlay. The remaining diagnostics on the page are there to show coverage, concentration, and composition quality, not to publish a second headline index.

Tenor pressure has been removed from both selection scoring and maintenance. The engine now keeps only a minimal operational roll near expiry instead of forcing the basket toward a target tenor or expiry preference profile.

9. Directionality And Risk-Up Semantics

Every non-cash position is directionally explicit. The product distinguishes the raw market YES probability from the basket’s Risk Price. If the basket is long a risk-up escalation market, it holds Long YES and the risk price equals market YES. If the basket is long a de-escalation market on the opposite side, it holds Long NO and the risk price equals 1 - Market YES.

  • Long YES: risk rises when the market YES probability rises.
  • Long NO: risk rises when the market YES probability falls because the market itself describes de-escalation, normalization, or peace.
  • Website contract tables therefore show Instruction, Market YES, Risk Price, and a short Why label.

Example: if a contract asks “Will Israel and Saudi Arabia normalize relations by March 31?”, the basket may hold Long NO. In that case the raw market YES can be low while the basket’s risk price is high, because failed normalization is treated as risk-up for the basket.

10. Basket Slot Architecture

Broad pattern matching alone was not strong enough for the highest-sensitivity geopolitical and energy baskets, so the engine now supports explicit slot schemas. Slot schemas are hard-coded thematic sub-buckets with per-slot caps, required-if-available rules, and proxy controls.

  • ADIT-S3: Iran-Israel escalation, Hamas ceasefire failure, Hezbollah/Lebanon, Red Sea/Hormuz disruption, US-Iran intervention, normalization failure.
  • ADIT-S2: Russia territorial advance, ceasefire failure, NATO direct clash, Greenland/Arctic/Nordic tension, nuclear/strategic escalation, EU/NATO security fracture.
  • ADIT-E3: Hormuz/Red Sea disruption, export-terminal/refinery hits, OPEC restriction, shipping disruption, producer shortfall, and capped oil-price proxy slots.

This slot architecture is what prevents ADIT-S2 from degenerating into a pile of near-zero tail contracts, and what prevents ADIT-E3 from becoming an empty or pure crude-ladder basket when direct disruption markets exist.

11. Level Construction And Performance Proxies

Two level paths are published. First, per-basket monthly level proxies from weighted contract prices plus cash sleeve. Second, an aggregate weighted index-level path across baskets. Each path is aligned with rebalance points and accompanied by entries/exits and turnover diagnostics.

Macro-factor overlays are computed from market-level beta exposures inherited from community factor models and aggregated by basket weights each month.

12. Governance Checks And Failure Modes

Quality control checks focus on coherence and leak prevention: no stale-expiry holdings after rebalance, no lookahead against inferred first-seen dates, no duplicate exclusivity groups per basket-month, and bounded position/cash constraints. If any check fails, artifacts make the failure visible at CSV level for audit and correction.

13. Reproducible Output Artifacts

The following outputs define the single operational workflow and are sufficient for third-party replication:

  • last_year_monthly_compositions.csv: full contract-level composition each month.
  • basket_level_monthly.csv: per-basket monthly level, cash, entries/exits, turnover.
  • aggregate_basket_level.csv: aggregate level path with rebalance-change diagnostics.
  • basket_inception_policy.csv: raw history start, automatic inception date, override, and effective dashboard start for each basket.
  • slot_coverage_diagnostics.csv: per-slot candidate and selected coverage by rebalance.
  • monthly_cash_positions.csv: monthly cash and risk context by basket.
  • contract_lifecycle_events.csv: entry/exit events with reasons.
  • rebalance_transitions.csv and rebalance_cost_model.csv: execution diagnostics.
